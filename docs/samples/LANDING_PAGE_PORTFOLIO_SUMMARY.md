## Context

A fictional NGO / education team has a rough idea for a youth AI literacy workshop, but the concept is not yet clear enough to build a website or launch a campaign.

The team needs a landing page prototype brief first, so stakeholders can understand:

- who the workshop is for
- why AI literacy matters
- what participants will do
- what safe and responsible AI use means
- what action the audience should take next

---

## Problem

- **Unclear audience:** the original idea says “students and people interested in AI,” but does not separate students, parents, teachers, youth workers, or NGO stakeholders.
- **Unclear value proposition:** the workshop needs a practical message that explains AI literacy without overpromising learning outcomes.
- **Privacy / responsible AI risk:** youth, SEN, education, and NGO contexts require conservative handling of student, service-user, parent, teacher, and programme data.

---

## My Role

- **AI PM / product planner:** translated a vague programme idea into audience, problem statement, CTA, scope boundary, and prototype structure.
- **Campaign message planner:** shaped landing page messaging, benefits, FAQ, and responsible AI positioning.
- **Client-safe AI workflow designer:** defined privacy safeguards, human review points, and limits on AI-assisted drafting.

---

## Key Product Decisions

| Decision | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Use `Register Interest` instead of direct registration | Avoids collecting personal data before consent, form design, storage, and approval are clear. |
| Prototype brief before production site | Aligns audience, message, CTA, privacy note, and scope before implementation. |
| Include responsible AI note | Makes safety and human review part of the page, not an afterthought. |
| Avoid real youth / SEN / student data | Reduces risk in NGO / education / social-service contexts. |
| Use FAQ to address parent / teacher concerns | Handles common questions about beginners, coding, accounts, privacy, and suitability. |
| Avoid hard claims about learning outcomes | Prevents misleading claims about academic results, conversion, sign-ups, or AI capability. |
