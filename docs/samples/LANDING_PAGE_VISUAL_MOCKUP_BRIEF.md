# 3. Page Sections

Design only 5 main sections to keep the mockup clean and portfolio-readable.

## Section 1: Hero

Purpose:

- Explain the workshop concept in one glance.
- Show that the page is beginner-friendly and safety-aware.
- Provide a clear CTA without collecting real personal data.

Suggested copy:

```text
Headline:
Help young people understand AI before they rely on it.

Subheadline:
A beginner-friendly AI literacy workshop concept for students, parents, teachers, and youth workers — focused on practical use, critical thinking, privacy, and responsible AI.

Primary CTA:
Register Interest

Secondary CTA:
View Workshop Outline
```

Layout notes:

- Left side: headline, short text, CTA buttons.
- Right side: abstract illustration or simple card stack showing `Question → Check → Review → Decide`.
- Add a small label: `Fictional portfolio prototype`.

Risk / privacy note:

- CTA must not link to a real form.
- Do not imply this is a real launched programme.

## Section 2: Problem

Purpose:

- Show why the workshop is needed.
- Translate vague programme idea into clear audience pain points.

Suggested copy:

```text
Young people are using AI tools, but many still need guidance on accuracy, privacy, overreliance, and responsible judgment.
```

Problem cards:

```text
1. Too much hype
AI is often presented as magic instead of a tool that needs human review.

2. Privacy uncertainty
Students may not know what personal or school data should never be uploaded.

3. Hard to discuss
Parents, teachers, and youth workers may lack shared language for AI literacy.
```

Layout notes:

- Use 3 simple cards.
- Keep each card short.
- Use icons only if licensed and generic.

Risk / privacy note:

- Do not include real stories, real students, real school examples, or real SEN scenarios.

## Section 3: Who It Is For

Purpose:

- Clarify target audience quickly.
- Show that the mockup considers multiple stakeholders.

Audience cards:

```text
Students
Curious about AI but need safe, practical guidance.

Parents
Want to understand how young people use AI responsibly.

Teachers / Youth Workers
Need beginner-friendly language for AI literacy discussion.
```

Layout notes:

- Use 3 audience cards.
- Avoid photos of real minors.
- Use abstract avatars or simple icons only.

Risk / privacy note:

- Do not use real school uniforms, classroom photos, or identifiable youth imagery.

## Section 4: Responsible AI Note

Purpose:

- Make privacy and responsible AI visible in the page, not hidden in a footer.
- Show client-safe AI workflow awareness.

Suggested copy:

```text
Responsible AI note
This workshop concept uses fake or public-safe examples only. Participants should not upload real personal, school, family, health, counselling, financial, identity, SEN, or service-user data into AI tools.
```

Layout notes:

- Use a callout box with a calm visual style.
- Place it before FAQ so safety is seen before action.
- Include a small human-review phrase: `AI output should be checked by people before use.`

Risk / privacy note:

- Do not imply legal or educational compliance is guaranteed.
- Add wording such as: `Real use requires organization policy and approval.`

## Section 5: FAQ + CTA

Purpose:

- Address parent / teacher concerns.
- End with a low-risk next step.

Suggested FAQ copy:

```text
Q1. Is this for beginners?
Yes. It is designed as an introductory AI literacy workshop concept.

Q2. Is this a coding workshop?
No. The focus is practical understanding, safe use, and critical thinking.

Q3. Will real student data be used?
No. The concept uses fake or public-safe examples only.
```

CTA footer:

```text
Interested in a youth AI literacy session?
[Register Interest]
Prototype only — no real registration form is connected.
```

Layout notes:

- Use accordion-style FAQ blocks or simple stacked cards.
- End with a CTA footer.
- Keep the fictional label visible near the footer.

Risk / privacy note:

- CTA does not collect real personal data.
- Screenshot should not imply live registration.

# 6. Visual Direction

## Tone

```text
Calm, practical, youth-friendly, education-focused, responsible, non-hype.
```

## Layout Style

- Clean landing page layout.
- Card-based sections.
- Clear CTA buttons.
- Large readable headline.
- Enough whitespace for portfolio screenshot readability.
- Avoid dense text blocks.

## Suggested Colors

Use plain, non-branded color direction:

- light neutral background
- soft blue or green accent for trust and learning
- warm highlight color for CTA
- high-contrast text for readability

Do not use exact real NGO, school, government, or OpenAI brand colors.

## Typography Direction

- Use simple sans-serif typography.
- Prioritize readability over decorative style.
- Use clear hierarchy: title, subtitle, section heading, card text.
- Avoid tiny footer text that cannot be read in portfolio screenshot.

## Icon / Illustration Style

- Use generic line icons or abstract shapes.
- Suggested icon themes: question, shield, checklist, conversation, lightbulb.
- Avoid realistic youth faces or identifiable classroom scenes.

## Accessibility Notes

- Maintain strong color contrast.
- Make CTA buttons large enough to see clearly.
- Keep text short and readable.
- Do not rely only on color to communicate risk or safety.
- Use headings that make sense when scanned.

## Mobile Readability Notes

- Desktop screenshot comes first, but content should stack naturally on mobile.
- Hero, Responsible AI note, and CTA should remain visible and readable in a mobile crop.
- Avoid 4-column layouts that collapse badly.

Branding restriction:

```text
Do not use OpenAI, real school, real NGO, real government, or real client branding.
```

# 7. Asset Safety Rules

- Use generic illustration or abstract shapes.
- Avoid real minors or school uniforms.
- Avoid identifiable classrooms.
- Avoid real NGO / school logos.
- Use only licensed Canva / Freepik / stock assets.
- Check font, image, icon, and music licensing if used.
- Competitor screenshots are for reference only, not to copy.
- Do not use real student photos, real activity photos, real workshop screenshots, or real testimonials.
- Do not create visuals that imply a real programme launch or real sign-up result.

If unsure, use abstract cards, shapes, and icons instead of people photography.
