# Portfolio Case Template

## Purpose

This template turns AI PM, IP building, digital marketing, consulting, campaign, or prototype work into a reusable AI PM portfolio case study.

It is designed to show product thinking, workflow design, research judgment, AI-assisted process, QA discipline, privacy awareness, and business value.

Use only fake, generic, public, or approved anonymized examples. Do not include real client data, private notes, participant information, credentials, or confidential business details.

Example:

```text
I have been turning AI PM Radar from a static content project into a documentation-first workflow system.

The goal is not to automate everything. The goal is to design a safer path from research and client-safe notes to useful outputs: audit templates, landing page briefs, content systems, and portfolio case studies.

One key decision was to keep human review at the center. AI can help summarize, structure, and generate drafts, but it should not decide what is true, what is safe to publish, or what a client should do without review.

This case helped me practice AI PM thinking: scope control, source quality, privacy rules, reusable workflow design, and business-facing documentation.

The next step is to test the templates with fake sample cases before using them in real client work.
```

## 18. 60-second Interview Pitch

```text
60-second pitch:

1. Context:

2. Problem:

3. What I did:

4. AI PM decision:

5. Result / output:

6. Limitation:

7. Why it matters for the role:
```

Example:

```text
AI PM Radar started as a static AI product and business radar for non-technical AI PM learners, NGO and education practitioners, and SME decision-makers.

After building the content workflow, I extended it into a documentation-first consulting workflow. The problem I wanted to solve was that many small teams want to use AI for content, proposals, and project documentation, but they lack privacy rules, human review points, and reusable templates.

I created templates for consulting audit, landing page planning, content repurposing, resource triage, and portfolio case generation. My key AI PM decision was to avoid full automation first and focus on a safe, human-in-the-loop workflow.

The output is not a finished SaaS product. It is a reusable workflow system that shows how I approach discovery, scope control, risk management, and client-safe AI adoption.

This matters for an AI PM role because it demonstrates practical product thinking: define the user problem, design the workflow, control risk, validate with templates, and document the case clearly.
```

```text

Limitations stated? yes / no
```
