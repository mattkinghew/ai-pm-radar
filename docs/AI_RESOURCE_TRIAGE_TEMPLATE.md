# AI Resource Triage Template

## Purpose

This template helps evaluate resources from X, Threads, GitHub, newsletters, blogs, AI tools, courses, reports, and product announcements.

The goal is to decide whether a resource should be used, tested, parked, or rejected for AI PM Radar, IP building, digital marketing, consulting, or portfolio case generation.

Use this before adding a resource to a workflow. Do not save sensitive client data, credentials, private notes, or unverified claims into public documentation.

## Scoring Guidance

Use a 1 to 5 score for each dimension.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Weak fit, high uncertainty, or low practical value |
| 2 | Some relevance, but unclear evidence or effort may be high |
| 3 | Useful with review, but not urgent |
| 4 | Strong fit, practical, and likely reusable |
| 5 | High-value resource with clear workflow or portfolio use |

### Evaluation Matrix

```text
AI PM relevance: 1-5
IP building relevance: 1-5
Digital marketing relevance: 1-5
Consulting relevance: 1-5
Evidence quality: 1-5
Ease of use: 1-5
Risk control: 1-5
Portfolio value: 1-5
```

### Suggested Decision Rule

```text
Use:
- high relevance
- clear evidence
- low to manageable risk
- direct output artifact is obvious

Test:
- promising but needs hands-on validation
- tool or workflow may be useful but capability is uncertain
- needs a small experiment before adoption

Park:
- useful later but not aligned with current priority
- high effort or dependency
- not enough time to evaluate now

Reject:
- unsourced claim
- high privacy / security / legal risk
- mostly hype
- no clear fit with AI PM, IP, marketing, consulting, or portfolio workflow
```

## Evidence Level Guide

| Evidence Level | Description | Typical Trust |
|---|---|---|
| Official source | Product blog, documentation, company announcement | High, but may be promotional |
| Primary research | Research paper, technical report, policy document | High, but may need interpretation |
| Reputable media | Established publication with named reporting | Medium to High |
| Expert commentary | Known practitioner explaining experience | Medium |
| Personal opinion | Individual post without supporting evidence | Low to Medium |
| Unsourced claim | No link, no evidence, no reproducible detail | Low |
| Unknown | Cannot verify origin | Very low |

## Required Effort Guide

| Effort | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Can be summarized or referenced quickly | Official blog post with clear update |
| Medium | Needs testing, comparison, or synthesis | New AI tool workflow or GitHub repo |
| High | Needs setup, data cleaning, prototype, or multiple sources | Agent framework, automation workflow, complex report |

## Risk Guide

### Accuracy Risk

Ask:

- Is the claim supported by source evidence?
- Is it a prediction rather than fact?
- Does it require technical testing?

### Privacy Risk

Ask:

- Does it require uploading client, student, participant, or internal data?
- Does it ask for access to Notion, Google Drive, Gmail, calendar, or private files?
- Can it be tested with dummy data instead?

### Security Risk

Ask:

- Does it require API keys, OAuth, browser extension access, or local file access?
- Does it ask to run unknown scripts?
- Does it affect deployment, credentials, or production data?

### Legal / Compliance Risk

Ask:

- Does it involve financial, medical, legal, HR, student, or social-service data?
- Does it require consent or organizational approval?
- Does the output risk being interpreted as professional advice?

### Overclaiming Risk

Ask:

- Does the resource promise unrealistic productivity, revenue, conversion, or automation results?
- Can the result be framed as a test, not a guarantee?

### Maintenance Risk

Ask:

- Will this create recurring work?
- Does it depend on a paid tool, unstable API, or complex setup?
- Is there a simple manual fallback?

## Example: AI Tool Resource

```text
Resource name: Example AI meeting summary tool
Source URL: https://example.com
Source type: AI tool
Date reviewed: YYYY-MM-DD
Reviewer: Matthew

Core idea:
Use AI to summarize meetings and extract action items.

Evidence level:
Official source / product marketing. Needs hands-on validation.

Useful for AI PM?
Yes. Supports meeting intelligence and requirement clarification.

Useful for IP building?
Maybe. Meeting insights can become anonymized pain point notes.

Useful for digital marketing?
Maybe. Can support campaign briefing after client meetings.

Useful for consulting?
Yes. Useful for documentation-heavy consulting workflow.

Required effort:
Medium. Need trial account, dummy meeting notes, and privacy review.

Cost / dependency:
Paid after trial. Account required.

Risk:
Accuracy risk: Medium; summaries may miss nuance.
Privacy risk: High if raw client meetings are uploaded.
Security risk: Medium; requires account integration.
Legal / compliance risk: Depends on meeting content and consent.
Overclaiming risk: Medium; should not be sold as automatic project management.
Maintenance risk: Medium; subscription and workflow dependency.

Can integrate into which workflow?
- consulting workflow
- campaign planning
- portfolio case study

Output artifact:
- meeting action template
- anonymized case note

Decision:
Test

Next action:
Run one dummy transcript through the workflow and compare against manual notes.
```
