# Digital Marketing Service Pack

## Purpose

This document defines three practical AI-assisted service packages that can be supported by the AI PM Radar documentation-first workflow.

The goal is to create clear consulting-style offers for IP building, campaign planning, landing page thinking, prototype planning, and portfolio case generation.

This is not aggressive sales copy. These packages should be used as internal planning references, proposal starting points, and portfolio evidence.

## Service Design Principles

- Start with business and audience problems, not AI tools.
- Use AI to accelerate research, structure, drafting, and review.
- Keep human review responsible for accuracy, tone, privacy, and final decisions.
- Use generic or anonymized examples unless client approval exists.
- Produce concrete artifacts that can become portfolio case notes.
- Avoid promising guaranteed growth, sales, virality, or conversion.

# Package 1: AI-assisted IP Content System

## Target Clients

- Solo consultants, trainers, coaches, educators, and creative professionals
- NGO / education programme teams that need clearer public communication
- SME founders who have expertise but lack a repeatable content workflow
- Personal brands that want to turn experience into structured content

## Client Pain Points

- Many raw ideas, notes, screenshots, PDFs, and meeting records, but no system to reuse them
- Content is created ad hoc and depends on mood or deadline pressure
- Expertise is not clearly packaged into public-facing themes
- AI tools are used for drafting, but outputs sound generic or risky
- Private client or participant information may be accidentally reused

## Deliverables

- IP theme map
- Source capture template
- Content repurposing template
- Weekly content workflow
- 2 to 4 content pillars
- Sample outputs for Threads, IG, LinkedIn, and carousel outline
- Privacy and claim-check rules
- Portfolio-ready workflow case note

## Process

1. Review existing content sources and business goals.
2. Define target audience and content pillars.
3. Create a source-to-output workflow.
4. Build reusable templates for capture, synthesis, and repurposing.
5. Produce sample content outputs from generic or approved source material.
6. Review risks, privacy rules, and publishing checklist.
7. Package the workflow as a repeatable content operating system.

## Estimated Effort Level

Medium.

Typical effort depends on the amount of existing material and how much client-sensitive information must be cleaned.

## Risks

- Client may expect AI to replace strategy and editorial judgment.
- Content may become too generic if the source material is weak.
- Private data may leak if capture rules are not followed.
- Overclaiming expertise or results can damage trust.

## Portfolio Value

Demonstrates:

- AI-assisted knowledge management
- content operations design
- privacy-aware workflow design
- human-in-the-loop quality control
- ability to turn expertise into reusable IP assets

## Suggested Next Step

Create a one-page audit using:

```text
Current content sources → target audience → content pillars → first 10 output ideas → risk rules
```

# Package 2: AI-assisted Campaign Proposal

## Target Clients

- NGOs and education teams preparing youth, inclusion, community, or training campaigns
- SMEs planning a small-scale product, service, or event campaign
- Creative teams that need proposal structure and stakeholder-ready materials
- Programme teams that need to turn a rough idea into a campaign brief

## Client Pain Points

- Campaign ideas are scattered across messages, decks, notes, and references
- Stakeholders agree on the theme but not the target audience or concrete actions
- Proposal writing takes too long and lacks structure
- Previous campaigns have content but not enough learning documentation
- Teams want to use AI, but worry about accuracy, privacy, or tone

## Deliverables

- Campaign brief
- Target audience and pain point summary
- Message hierarchy
- Channel plan
- Content angle list
- Draft timeline
- Risk and assumption log
- Stakeholder questions list
- Optional: landing page or carousel content outline
- Portfolio-ready campaign planning case note

## Process

1. Collect sanitized campaign context and reference materials.
2. Define audience, objective, constraints, and success signals.
3. Turn raw ideas into campaign angles and message hierarchy.
4. Draft proposal sections using AI-assisted structure.
5. Review feasibility, risk, tone, and stakeholder alignment.
6. Prepare a final campaign planning document.
7. Convert the work into an anonymized portfolio case note.

## Estimated Effort Level

Medium to High.

Effort is higher when stakeholders are unclear, source materials are messy, or the campaign requires visual planning.

## Risks

- AI may produce generic campaign ideas without local context.
- Stakeholder expectations may exceed budget or timeline.
- Public claims may require evidence or approval.
- Sensitive community, youth, SEN, or service-user contexts require extra care.
- The proposal may be mistaken for a guaranteed performance plan.

## Portfolio Value

Demonstrates:

- campaign problem framing
- stakeholder communication
- AI-assisted proposal drafting
- audience and message design
- risk-aware project planning
- ability to turn campaign work into reusable consulting evidence

## Suggested Next Step

Create a campaign one-pager:

```text
Objective → audience → pain point → message → channels → timeline → risks → next decision
```

# Package 3: Landing Page / Demo Prototype Sprint

## Target Clients

- Consultants, educators, NGOs, and SMEs testing a new service idea
- Teams that need a simple landing page before committing to a full website
- AI workflow builders who need a demo prototype for stakeholder discussion
- Portfolio builders who need a small but explainable product artifact

## Client Pain Points

- The service idea is hard to explain in one page
- Existing materials are too scattered for a clear landing page
- Stakeholders want to see something concrete before approving next steps
- Team is unsure whether to build a website, deck, form, or prototype
- AI-generated copy may sound exaggerated or unclear

## Deliverables

- Landing page angle
- Problem / audience / value proposition draft
- Page section outline
- CTA proposal
- Proof or evidence checklist
- Risk / disclaimer note
- Optional low-fidelity wireframe
- Optional static demo prototype plan
- Portfolio-ready prototype planning case note

## Process

1. Clarify target audience, offer, and intended action.
2. Extract source material from notes, interviews, PDFs, or existing content.
3. Draft landing page structure.
4. Define CTA and proof requirements.
5. Review claims, privacy, and feasibility.
6. Create a simple prototype plan or static page brief.
7. Document the decision logic as a case study.

## Estimated Effort Level

Medium.

Effort becomes High if the client expects design implementation, copywriting, analytics setup, or frontend development in the same sprint.

## Risks

- Landing page may overpromise if proof is weak.
- Prototype may be mistaken for a finished production system.
- Scope may expand into branding, website build, automation, and analytics.
- Client may request real user data collection without proper privacy planning.
- Technical demo may create maintenance expectations.

## Portfolio Value

Demonstrates:

- product thinking
- offer validation
- landing page information architecture
- AI-assisted copy and UX planning
- scope control between concept, prototype, and production
- digital transformation consulting workflow

## Suggested Next Step

Create a landing page brief:

```text
Audience → painful problem → why now → proposed solution → proof needed → CTA → risk note
```

## Cross-Package Governance Checklist

Before delivering any package:

- Confirm what is public, internal, or sensitive.
- Remove client names and personal data unless explicit approval exists.
- Mark assumptions and unverified claims.
- Avoid legal, medical, financial, or guaranteed performance advice.
- Keep a clear boundary between draft, proposal, prototype, and production system.
- Store final artifacts in `docs/` or an approved documentation space.
- Turn each project into a short portfolio case note after delivery.
